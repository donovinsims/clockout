# Product Marketing Context

_Last updated: June 18, 2026_

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Done-for-you automation for local service businesses.
**What it does:** I audit a trade shop's lead-to-cash workflow in 48 hours, identify every revenue leak (quantified in dollars), then build custom automations inside the tools they already use to stop those leaks. Full build in 7 days. Total engagement: 9 days. Customer owns everything.
**Product category:** Business automation / workflow automation for home service trades.
**Product type:** Service (done-for-you automation builds) — not SaaS.
**Business model:** One-time flat fee ($497 beta, $1,494 standard). No subscription. No retainer.

**Decision-makers:** Owner-operator (works in the field, runs the business, too busy to fix systems).

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Local service businesses are losing 20–35% of revenue to predictable operational leaks — missed calls, slow quotes, dropped follow-ups, unasked-for reviews.
**Why alternatives fall short:**

- Field service software (ServiceTitan): Handles dispatch/invoicing but not lead response or multi-channel follow-up
- Marketing agencies / AI consultants: $500–$5K/month retainer, rented system, generic templates
- Hiring a VA: Needs management, burns out, no system underneath
- DIY automation: Requires time and skill the owner doesn't have

**What it costs them:** $5,400/month from 3 missed calls/week. $12K+ single roofing job lost to slow follow-up. 20–35% of unsigned quotes unrecovered. 2 hrs/day per tech lost to admin.
**Emotional tension:** Sunday night dread. Mental list while trying to sleep. Knowing exactly where the money leaks but being too busy to fix it. Resignation that "this is just how it is."

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Other automation consultants and local marketing agencies selling AI/automation services — fall short because of monthly pricing ($500–$5K/month), rented (not owned) systems, generic templates, slow setup (2–6 weeks), and incentive to keep you on retainer.
**Secondary:** Field service software (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber), GoHighLevel — fall short because they still require someone to configure and manage them, don't handle phone calls (the #1 leak), and don't build the system for you.
**Indirect:** Hiring a VA, DIY automation (Make/Zapier/n8n), doing nothing — fall short because they either require ongoing management, technical skill, or accept the leaks as normal.
