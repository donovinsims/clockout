# Clockout — Funnel & Sales Process

Internal reference for how a stranger becomes a paying client at Clockout — the end-to-end funnel, the audit-call talk track, the objection library, the follow-up emails, and where the funnel leaks. Reuse as context for the founder (Donovin) and AI agents. Plain founder voice; reuse the term **"Money-Leak Map"** everywhere.

## The funnel, end to end

The audit IS the call — a live 20-minute diagnosis, not a no-call PDF. The written Map is the takeaway *after*, because Donovin can't diagnose a shop he hasn't researched yet.

1. **Site CTA — "Find the Money I'm Losing →"** Sitewide button (hero may keep "Get Your 10 Hours Back →"). Subtext: *"Free 20-minute leak audit. Pick a time — I'll show you exactly where you're losing money."*
2. **/assessment short form.** Name, Business, Trade, Email, Phone + preferred contact (call/text), and "What's costing you the most right now?" Team size optional. Single column, labels visible. The page states the next step is a quick call *before* they fill it out — no bait-and-switch.
3. **Redirect to cal.com/donovin (prefilled).** On submit, details pass through so nothing is retyped. Cal.com sends confirmation + reminders.
4. **Prospect books a 20-minute call.** This is the audit.
5. **Donovin researches the business beforehand.** Earns the right to show real numbers.
6. **The call — leaks shown live, in dollars,** using the prospect's own numbers.
7. **~1 day later: the written Money-Leak Map.** Top leaks ranked + the first fix. Theirs to keep, hire him or not.
8. **Optional Build — $497** (you own it; 10-Hour Guarantee), chosen only after value is shown.
9. **Optional Operator OS concierge — $750/mo** (Two-Week Guarantee), an open invitation after the Build, never forced.

## The no-forced-upsell principle

The free call + the kept Map are the reciprocity gift — the give *is* the sell. The Build is a standalone, you-own-it purchase, offered only after the prospect sees the dollar value. The concierge is an optional continuation chosen *after* the Build, never bolted on the moment someone pays. Price comes after interest, never cold. Real scarcity is honest: **8 founding spots, 3 left.**

## The 20-minute audit-call talk track

1. **Open (1 min) — set the frame.** "This is the audit, not a pitch. By the end you'll know exactly where you're losing money, and you keep the plan either way."
2. **Discovery (5 min) — get the numbers.** Average ticket? Calls/week going to voicemail? What happens to a quote after it's sent — who follows up? Their numbers do the selling.
3. **Reveal the leaks in dollars (7 min).** "You're missing ~X calls/week. At your $___ ticket, that's ~$____/mo. Quotes go cold because nothing chases them — another $____." Their money, their math.
4. **Bridge (2 min).** "Here's the first thing I'd fix, and why it's first." One priority, not a feature dump.
5. **Soft close — two doors (3 min).** "Take this plan and run it yourself — no hard feelings. Or I build it with you, you own all of it, $497 while I'm taking founding clients. Three missed calls a month already covers it." Price comes *after* interest.
6. **If "let me think" →** "Fair — you keep the plan. Want me to hold a founding spot for a week?" Soft scarcity, no pressure.

## Objection library

| They say | Real concern | Response | Proof | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Too expensive." | Won't pay off | "Three recovered calls a month cover it — after that it's profit." | Their own leak number | "What's one job worth to you?" |
| "Let me think." | Fear of a bad call | "Keep the plan and use it either way. Only the founding price expires." | The free Map in hand | "What's the part you want to think over?" |
| "Will the AI replace my people / sound robotic?" | Reputation + identity | "It catches the calls already going to voicemail. Your people still do the work." | Demo the missed-call text-back | "How many after-hours calls do you lose now?" |
| "I tried automation before." | Been burned | "You tried a tool. This is built around your shop — and the 10-Hour Guarantee." | The guarantee | "What broke last time?" |
| "No time to set it up." | Effort | "That's the point — done-with-you, built in days. A couple short calls from you." | "Built in days, not weeks." | "Got 20 minutes this week?" |
| "Why pay monthly?" | Lock-in fear | "The build is yours forever. Concierge is optional — cancel any month, keep everything." | "You own it all." | "Want to start with just the build?" |
| "Just email me info." | Brush-off | "Will do — but the call is where you see your real numbers." | The personalized Map | "Worst case, you get a free plan — when works?" |

## Follow-up emails

**A. Post-audit** — send right after the call, Map attached.
> **Subject:** Your Money-Leak Map — [Business]
> Hey [First name] — good talking. Attached is your Money-Leak Map: where [Business] is leaking the most, ranked, with the first fix. Yours to keep and run, whether or not we work together. Want me to build the fixes? You own everything, no contract, 10 hours a week back in 30 days or I keep working — just reply "build it" and I'll send times. — Donovin

**B. No-show re-book** — send within an hour of a missed call.
> **Subject:** Missed you — still want your Money-Leak Map?
> Hey [First name] — we had a time set for your free leak audit and I didn't want to lose you. No stress if it got busy. Still happy to spend 20 minutes showing you where [Business] is leaking money and hand you the Map — no pitch. Grab a new time: cal.com/donovin — Donovin

## Where the funnel leaks (and how it's plugged)

- **The form → cal.com seam.** The redirect is the drop-off risk. Plugged by prefilling details (no retyping) and embedding/handing straight to the calendar.
- **No-shows.** Plugged by cal.com confirmation + reminders, and **email B** within an hour of a missed call.
- **Post-call silence.** Plugged by **email A** with the kept Map and a low-friction "reply 'build it'" — value first, then a soft door.
