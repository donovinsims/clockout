# Competitive & Positioning

Internal/agent reference for how Clockout is positioned against the alternatives, the wedge that makes it a category-of-one, and how to talk about competitors honestly. Anchor to this; do not contradict or invent numbers.

## The one-line positioning

Done-for-you AI workflow automation for local-service trades — start with a free audit, pay once to get it built, and **own everything I build**. Keep me on as your concierge only if it's worth it. No contracts, cancel anytime.

## The wedge: you own it / no lock-in

The villain is **not "monthly."** The villain is **lock-in and rented software you never own.** Most agencies rent you a system on a 12-month contract and keep it the day you stop paying. Clockout inverts that: every login and every line of code is yours, it keeps running if you cancel, and there's no contract holding you in.

That single move is what lets Clockout sell a recurring concierge ($750/mo Operator OS) while staying genuinely anti-agency. We're not against recurring revenue — we're against _trapped_ customers. The recurring relationship has to earn its place every month, because the customer can walk and keep everything.

## Clockout's durable edges (category-of-one)

- **A FREE audit that proves value before any spend.** Risk is 20 minutes, not a dime. This is the trust mechanism — it carries the sale instead of a pitch.
- **You OWN the system.** It keeps running if you stop paying. No one else in the comparable set offers this.
- **Local + done-with-you.** Not an offshore VA, not a faceless agency. Roscoe IL founder, on a call with you.
- **One-time build option** — no forced subscription. The concierge is an _optional continuation_, never a required step.
- **Honest, plain, no-lock-in voice.** No hype words, no exclamation points, real comparables only — which is exactly what converts skeptical trades owners.
- **ROI framing that reads as breakeven.** Three missed calls a week at a $450 ticket is ~$5,400/mo walking out the door; the recurring fee reads as breakeven in month one.

## The models behind the offer (internal reference)

Clockout adapts two proven playbooks:

- **Nick Ponte — foot-in-the-door demo-site wedge.** For no-website prospects, lead with a real built asset (a demo site) rather than a pitch. The asset funnels them into the audit. _Clockout use:_ the no-website segment gets a demo site as its entry ramp; everyone else enters straight through the audit. The website is a segment-specific ramp, not the homepage's main message.
- **Corey Ganim — AI Concierge model.** Paid assessment → recurring done-with-you concierge: ~biweekly build calls + unlimited async + a visible "what we built" log; floor ~$1k/mo for high-touch; raise the price with each easy yes. _Clockout adapts:_ free audit (during beta) → optional one-time Build → optional Operator OS concierge. The shared "what we've built for you" log is the renewal engine — it makes "you own everything" literally demonstrable every month.

The composite funnel: **free audit (front door) → Build (standalone, you own it) → open invitation to Operator OS (optional, month-to-month).** The Build is never a forced upsell; the concierge is chosen _after_ value is delivered.

## How to talk about competitors (without bashing)

- **Don't bash.** Anchor on **ownership + no-lock-in + ROI** (recovered missed calls). Let the free audit and the guarantees carry the trust.
- Name the real villain — **rented software you never own and 12-month lock-in** — not "monthly," because Clockout charges monthly too.
- Frame field-service software fairly: great tools, but they're _tools the owner still has to run_. Clockout does the running.
- Acknowledge "the next guy" honestly: the fastest phone wins, which is exactly the leak the Build closes.
- Use real comparables only. Never write "$X value" without a named, defensible comparable.

Until those exist, the free audit and the named guarantees (the 10-Hour Guarantee on the Build; the Two-Week Guarantee on Operator OS) do the trust-building. **Never fabricate testimonials, ratings, or case studies.**
