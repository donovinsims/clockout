# ICP & Market

*Who Clockout is for, who it isn't, and what the market will actually pay. Reusable business context for the founder and AI agents.*

## The ICP in one line

Owner-operated local-service businesses, **1–10 employees**, **~$500K–$5M revenue**, where the owner is hands-on, slammed, and can *name* their leaks (missed calls, slow quotes, cold follow-ups) but has no time to fix them.

## Firmographics

| Attribute | Fit |
|---|---|
| Ownership | Owner-operated, independent (owner is in the business daily) |
| Headcount | 1–10 employees |
| Revenue | ~$500K–$5M |
| Primary trades | HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, real estate, property management |
| Secondary trades | Landscaping, cleaning |
| Already spends | $500–$2,000/mo on recurring tools (FSM software, Angi, Google LSA) |

## The owner's mindset (psychographics)

- **Time-poor, not clueless.** They already know where money leaks out — missed calls, quotes that take days, leads that go cold. They just can't stop running jobs long enough to fix the systems.
- **Burned before.** Many have paid for software they never used or an agency that locked them in and disappeared. They're skeptical of slick pitches and "another monthly fee."
- **Owns the phone.** Decisions are made by one person; no committee, no procurement. A direct, honest, no-hype conversation lands.
- **ROI-literal.** They think in tickets and jobs. "Recover 3 missed calls a month" beats "improve efficiency."

## The two segments — and how each enters the funnel

Segmentation happens at **outreach**, not on the website. Both segments climb the same ladder: free 20-min audit → "Money-Leak Map" → $497 Build (you own it) → optional $750/mo Operator OS.

- **NO-WEBSITE leads (hottest "needs help" signal).** No website = an obvious, dollarized gap. **Wedge = a real demo site** built for them, no price quoted cold. The demo funnels them into the free audit. Channel: phone/SMS + DM (these leads rarely have email).
- **HAS-WEBSITE leads.** Lead straight with **the assessment** — the free 20-min leak audit. No demo-site step; the site is already there, so the entry point is the diagnosis.

## Geography

- **Remote:** anywhere in the US during beta.
- **Local area codes:** 779 / 815 (Stateline) and 312 / 773 / 847 (Chicago metro) — useful as a local-business signal.

## Qualifying signals (a good-fit lead)

- **Owner-named business** ("Dee's Plumbing," "Botti Realty") — signals owner-operated, not a chain.
- **Local area code** (779/815/312/773/847) — real local operator, not a national number.
- **25–400 Google reviews** — enough to be established, not so many it's an enterprise/franchise.
- **4.0+ star rating** — a real business that cares about its reputation.
- **Independent** — not a franchise, not multi-region.

## Anti-personas (explicit — do NOT pursue)

- **Enterprise** and large organizations.
- **SaaS / product companies** (Clockout serves service businesses, not software makers).
- **National chains & franchises:** HomeAdvisor, ServiceMaster, Servpro, Angi, RE/MAX, Keller Williams, etc.
- **1-800 / toll-free numbers** (national, not local).
- **Multi-region operators.**
- **Price-shoppers** who just want a $19/mo chatbot.
- **Owners retiring within ~2 years** (no incentive to build compounding systems).

## Willingness to pay (Rockford market, sourced)

These owners **already tolerate $500–$2,000/mo recurring** — monthly fees aren't foreign to them; *invisible* monthly value is the enemy.

| Comparable spend | Monthly range |
|---|---|
| FSM software — Housecall Pro | $149–614 |
| FSM software — ServiceTitan (5 techs) | ~$1,225–1,990 |
| Angi | $600–2,500 |
| Google Local Services Ads (LSA) | $500–2,000 |
| Automation / GHL agency retainers | $297–2,500 |

So **$750–$1,500/mo is mid-market**, even below specialized agencies. Clockout's $750/mo Operator OS sits comfortably inside what this owner already pays.

### The ROI-anchor rule

Rockford cost-of-living runs **~14–16% below national**, which compresses willingness-to-pay. **Any price above ~$1,000/mo needs an explicit ROI anchor**, framed in the owner's own units:

> Recover **3 missed calls/mo** at a **$450 ticket = $1,350 recovered** — so a $750–$1,500/mo retainer reads as breakeven (or better) in month one.

- **Easy yes:** ~$497–797/mo.
- **Achievable with the ROI story:** $1,200–1,500/mo.
- **Don't open here:** $2,200+/mo.

Lead with the recovered-calls math before naming any recurring price. Retention tracks **visible dollar outcomes**, not features.
