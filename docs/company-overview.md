# Clockout — Company Overview

> Reusable business context for the founder and AI agents. Internal reference — accurate and structured, not marketing copy.

## One-line positioning

Done-for-you AI workflow automation for local-service trades — you own everything you build, with no contracts and no lock-in.

## What Clockout is

Clockout builds and installs revenue-recovery automation for local-service businesses (HVAC, roofing, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, cleaning, property management, real estate). The core problem it attacks: small trade operators leak money and time on the unglamorous operational gaps that enterprise companies solved years ago — missed calls, slow quote follow-up, no review engine, dormant past customers.

The model is built around ownership, not rental. The client keeps every login and every line of code. There are no contracts and no forced subscription — the contrast is deliberate against rented SaaS / GoHighLevel stacks ($797–1,497/mo) and full marketing agencies ($2,200–5,000/mo), where the operator pays forever and owns nothing.

## The founder — Donovin Sims

Solo founder, based in Roscoe, IL. Clockout is a one-person operation at beta stage.

Donovin's authority comes from running operations at enterprise scale:

- **Uber** — operations for live events of 50,000+ attendees, including real-time dispatch.
- **Walgreens** — operations at national retail scale.

That pedigree is the spine of the positioning: the same operational discipline that ran high-volume, real-time logistics for large companies, brought down to Main Street. Donovin focuses on local-service trades specifically because these owners face the exact leaks enterprises engineered away — but they were never handed the playbook.

## Why it exists (origin)

Enterprise companies solved missed calls, slow follow-up, and lapsed-customer reactivation long ago — with systems, dispatch, and automation. Local contractors never had access to that playbook; they run on memory, sticky notes, and "I'll call them back." Clockout exists to port that enterprise operational layer to the small trade shop, in a form the owner actually owns.

## Mission

Hand local-service owners the operational systems big companies already use — recovering the revenue and hours they're losing — and let them own the result outright, free of contracts, lock-in, and rented-forever software.

1. **Free 20-min revenue-leak audit call** — a live diagnosis, not a sales pitch. Funnel CTA: "Find the Money I'm Losing →"; homepage hero CTA: "Get Your 10 Hours Back →".
2. **The "Money-Leak Map"** — a written, ranked list of leaks plus the first fix. Theirs to keep whether or not they hire Clockout.
3. **THE BUILD — $497 beta** (reg. $1,494, one-time; client owns everything). Includes: 24/7 missed-call text-back + AI front desk, quote follow-up engine, auto Google-review engine, past-customer reactivation, owner dashboard, and 30-day tuning. Backed by the **10-Hour Guarantee**.
4. **OPERATOR OS CONCIERGE — $750/mo founding rate** (cancel anytime, own everything). Biweekly build sessions + on-call access + a running build log. Backed by the **Two-Week Guarantee**. **Optional — never forced.** It's an invitation after the build, not a step the client is pushed into.
