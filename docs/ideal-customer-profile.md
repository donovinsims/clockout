## Target Market Overview

**Primary market:** Owner-operated local service businesses in Northern Illinois & Southern Wisconsin.

**Company characteristics:**
- 1–10 employees (often owner + a few field techs)
- Revenue: $500K–$5M
- Owner is hands-on, often works in the field alongside their team
- Currently using (or paying for) field service software like ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, or similar
- Losing revenue to operational leaks they can see but can't fix — missed calls, slow quotes, cold follow-ups, unasked-for reviews
- Too busy running the business to fix the systems that run the business

**Geography:** Rockford metro, Roscoe, Loves Park, Machesney Park, Beloit, Janesville, and surrounding Northern IL / Southern WI. Remote builds available anywhere in the US during beta.

## Jobs to Be Done

When a trade shop owner "hires" Clockout, they're hiring for these jobs:

1. **"Stop losing jobs I should have won"** — Missed calls, slow quote responses, and cold leads that competitors pick up because I didn't follow up fast enough.
2. **"Get my evenings and weekends back"** — The admin work (scheduling, quoting, follow-ups, invoices) that spills into nights and Sundays because there's no time during the workday.
3. **"Get my customers to come back and refer me"** — The maintenance renewals, repeat business, and reviews that I know I should capture but never have a system for.
4. **"Stop worrying that my business falls apart when I'm not there"** — The backup brain that keeps leads from dying and customers from leaving when I'm on a roof, on vacation, or asleep.
5. **"Make the tools I already pay for actually work together"** — The CRM, dispatch software, and phone system that are supposed to solve these problems but don't talk to each other.

## Pain Points

### Core Problem
Local service businesses are losing 20–35% of their revenue to operational leaks they can see but can't fix. Every leak is a known, predictable pattern — missed calls, slow quotes, dropped follow-ups, unasked-for reviews, churned maintenance customers.

### Why Current Solutions Fall Short
- **Field service software (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro):** Great for dispatch and invoicing, but doesn't handle lead response, follow-up cadence, or multi-channel communication. Still requires someone to be at a desk.
- **Marketing agencies:** Sell monthly retainers for AI front desks and chatbot installations — expensive ($500–$5K/month), ongoing commitment, and you're renting the system.
- **Hiring a VA:** Needs to be told what to do, when to do it, and what to do when the customer doesn't reply. High turnover. Works best on top of a system, not instead of one.
- **DIY automation:** Requires technical skill, ongoing maintenance, and time the owner doesn't have.

### What It Costs Them
- **$5,400/month** in lost revenue from just 3 missed calls/week at $450 average ticket
- **$12,000+** single storm-replacement roofing job lost to a slow follow-up
- **20–35%** of unsigned quotes that could be recovered with automated follow-up
- **30%** churn in first 6 months for recurring service businesses
- **2 hours/day** per tech lost to admin noise — 25% of productive time

### Emotional Tension
- **Stress:** Sunday night dread, the mental list that runs while trying to fall asleep
- **Frustration:** Knowing exactly where the money is leaking but being too busy to fix it
- **Fear:** Worrying the business falls apart when they're not watching it
- **Resignation:** "That's just how it is in this industry" — accepting the leaks as normal

## Anti-Persona (Who is NOT a good fit)

- **Enterprise businesses** with dedicated ops teams and existing automation stacks
- **SaaS companies** or product businesses (we serve local service trades)
- **Businesses outside the US** (remote builds work but US-only during beta)
- **Owners who don't want to change anything** — if the owner isn't willing to modify their workflow slightly, the system won't stick
- **Shops that already have a functioning end-to-end automation system** (rare, but they exist)
- **Anyone expecting a quick-fix chatbot** — Clockout is a full workflow build, not a plug-in
