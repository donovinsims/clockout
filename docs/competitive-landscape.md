## Competitive Tiers

### Direct Competitors
*Same solution category, same problem — other automation consultants and local marketing agencies selling AI / automation services to local service businesses.*

These competitors offer similar "done-for-you automation" packages, often bundled into monthly retainers. Many are marketing agencies that have added AI front desk / chatbot services as an offering.

**How they fall short:**
- **Monthly pricing:** $500–$5,000/month, locked into annual contracts. Clockout is $497 one-time.
- **Rented system:** The automation lives on their platform. If they go under, your system dies. Clockout hands over every credential.
- **Generic templates:** Agency "AI solutions" are often pre-built chatbots bolted on with minimal customization. Clockout maps the actual workflow first.
- **Slow setup:** Agencies quote 2–6 weeks for setup. Clockout delivers in 7 days.
- **Login proliferation:** Yet another dashboard for the owner to check. Clockout installs inside existing tools.
- **High churn incentive:** Agencies profit when you stay on the retainer. Clockout profits when the build works and you're done.

### Secondary Competitors
*Different solution, same problem — field service software and CRMs that claim to solve these issues.*

- **ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber:** Great for dispatch, scheduling, invoicing. But they don't handle lead response speed, multi-channel communication, or automated follow-up cadence. Still requires a human at a desk.
- **GoHighLevel (GHL):** Powerful marketing/automation platform, but requires configuration and ongoing management. Most trade shop owners don't have time to set it up properly.
- **Kajabi / ClickFunnels:** Not trade-specific. Requires significant DIY effort.

**How they fall short:**
- All require ongoing configuration and management
- No one builds the system for you
- The owner still has to figure out what to automate and how
- Most don't handle phone calls (the #1 leak point)

### Indirect Competitors
*Conflicting approach to the same problem.*

- **Hiring a virtual assistant:** Cheaper upfront ($400–$800/month overseas) but high turnover, requires management, and doesn't build a system.
- **DIY automation (Make, Zapier, n8n):** Powerful but requires technical skill and maintenance. Owner doesn't have the time.
- **Doing nothing:** The default. Owner accepts the leaks as "just how it is in this industry."
