# About Donovin

## Opening

I grew up in Roscoe. Hononegah High, baseball at NIU, summers running a shovel for the Winnebago County Highway Department. Northern Illinois is home.

Then I left for ten years.

## The big-shop years

At Uber I ran operations for major live events across the US and Canada — venues with fifty thousand people inside them, real-time dispatch, zero-margin operations. The job was making sure a missed handoff at gate 12 didn't cascade into chaos at gate 1.

At Walgreens I built and shipped digital products at national retail scale. Different industry. Same problem class. When the system breaks, the customer leaves.

The patterns are identical. A missed call, a slow quote, a follow-up that never went out — at Uber it cost a ride. At a roofing shop in Loves Park, it costs a $12,000 job.

## Why I came back

The local trades around here are getting beat by software companies in California that have never met them. The shops that built Rockford, Roscoe, Beloit — they're losing leads to slicker competitors because nobody taught them the playbook that the big companies use.

I know that playbook. I'm bringing it home, applied to one shop at a time, at a flat price you can actually afford.

## What that looks like in practice

I take one job and trace it through your business — phone tree, CRM, dispatch, invoice. I find where money is leaking. I quantify it in dollars, not in adjectives. Then I build the fix inside the tools you already use and hand you every login when it's done.

No contracts. Cancel anytime. You own everything.

If it doesn't give you back ten hours a week inside thirty days, I keep working until it does. Free.
